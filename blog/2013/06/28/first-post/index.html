Proof That I Am, or May Have Been in the Past, Both a Geek and a Nerd

OK, I publicly admit…

  • My bedside nightstand is an SGI Crimson with RealityEngine graphics (alas with only a single RM4, but it isn’t rendering anything these days anyway).
  • My house’s core gigabit switch is connected to my living room switch via a single-mode fibre backbone.
  • I earned a gold medal (summa cum laude) for my first-year National Latin Exam and a silver medal (magna cum laude) for my second-year exam. For the third year I guess I was lazy, as I didn’t place.
  • I grew up watching the original Doctor Who series and liked it (insert favorite Doctor here).
  • I used to attend science fiction conventions, but I feel better now–and Bill Shatner really is like that in person.
  • I use Emacs and I like it.

…but I don’t…

  • read “graphic novels.”
  • play video games.
  • have any iThings.
  • choose a side in the editor war; I use vi extensively (yeah, in most cases it’s really vim, but I don’t use any of vim’s extra features because I learned real vi on real Unix), and besides, ed is the standard text editor.

…but yet I do have…

My vintage computers

I keep hanging onto these things:

  • Aforementioned SGI Crimson, 150 MHz R4400, 256 MiB RAM, RealityEngine graphics, and a couple of dual-port fast-wide differential SCSI controller cards
  • SGI Indigo, 150 MHz R4400, 64 MiB RAM, XZ graphics
  • SGI Indigo, 100 MHz R4000, 32 MiB RAM, XS24Z graphics
  • Sun SPARCstation 20 Model 712, 384 MiB RAM, ZX and on-board 4 MiB SX framebuffers, SunVideo SBus card
  • Sun SPARCstation 10 Model 51 (I misplaced the other SM51 MBus module and haven’t found it yet), 192 MiB RAM, CG6 framebuffer
  • Sun SPARCstation LX, 96 MiB RAM, on-board CG6 framebuffer (I think it has the VRAM expansion but can’t be bothered to check because it serves as a monitor stand right now), Big Mac ethernet SBus card
  • Apple Macintosh Quadra 950, 64 MiB RAM, 2 MiB VRAM
  • IBM PS/2 Model 95 (8595)–with the cool front-panel display–i486DX4/100, 8 MiB RAM, quaint token ring card, and a couple of 10 Mbps ethernet cards
  • Commodore 64

Honorary mentions of past exotica that was sold off while there was still some slight demand:

  • NeXTstation Mono, 8 MiB RAM
  • Sun 3/80, 8 MiB RAM
  • Sun 386i/250, 8 MiB RAM
